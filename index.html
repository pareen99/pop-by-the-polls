Pop by the Polls

This is a short research article for the 2020 general elections. It covers four issues currently being discussed, a candidate running for President of the United States, a polling place, and a sample ballot.

Issues

These are the issues that voters and candidates are discussing this general season.

COVID-19 Mask Mandate

61% of the population agrees that masks should be mandated indoors or in crowded areas, but there was no law to enfore this.

Drug Price Regulation

The majority of Americans believe medicine and healthcare should be socialized.

Climate Change

This year alone has shown the impact of climate change and therefore majority Americans believe incentive should be provided for altermate energy production.

Gun Control

This contraversial issue sees the nation split almost exactly in half. Many believe ther should be restrictions, testing and training but others would rather not.

Candidate

This is a person that is running to be nominated as a general election candidate for President of the United States.

Joe Biden

Joe Biden is the Former Vice President to Barack Obama. His Vice President is Kamala Harris. Biden strongly believes in Medicare for the nation and renewable energy programs. He also will not tolerrate white supremacists and shows more interest in the Black Lives Matter movement than President Trump. Biden has served the public for most of his life and has worked under Obama, therfore, he proves to be a well balance and well experienced candidate.

Polling Place

This is a location that voters can visit to cast a ballot for the candidates they prefer.

Brassfield Elementary School

Brassfield Elementary Schools is located in Raleigh North Carolina. The location is convinietn as it is only 5 to 10 minutes from where I stay. As a school it is neutral ground and is big enough for all the voters to be socially ditsanced as well. The polling place opened on the October 15th 2020. On weekedays it is open from 8am to 7.30pm, on Saturday it is open from 8am to 3pm and Sunday from 1pm to 5pm. The polling place will be open till October 31st.

Sample Ballot

This is a document that potential voters can use to practice voting and to preview which candidates are up for election.

Sample Ballot, Wake County, North Carolina

The ballot was fairly easy to find through Vote.org. After selecting your state, the polling place locator will show you the closest location as well as a smaple ballot. The ballot instructions are very clear and the layout clearly marks each section. For Wake County offices, the Board of Commissioners District 2 only has one candidate running.
